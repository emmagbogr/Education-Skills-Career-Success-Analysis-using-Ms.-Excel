## üìå Project Overview

This project analyzes the relationship between **educational background, skills development, and career success outcomes** using a structured dataset of 400 individuals. The analysis focuses on how factors such as **field of study, certifications, soft skills, and demographics** influence **job level attainment** and **starting salaries**.

The project demonstrates end-to-end data analytics skills using **Microsoft Excel**, including data understanding, analysis, visualization, and insight generation.

## üéØ Objectives

The analysis seeks to answer the following questions:

* What is the total student population and gender distribution?
* How many certifications are obtained (total, min, avg, max)?
* What is the distribution of soft skills scores?
* Which fields of study dominate Entry, Mid, and Senior job levels?
* How is the workforce distributed across job levels?
* What are the average starting salaries by field of study?

## üìà Key Insights

* The student population is **perfectly gender-balanced** (200 male, 200 female).
* A total of **1,050 certifications** were obtained, with an average of **3 per individual**.
* **65%** of individuals occupy **mid-level roles**, while only **15%** are in senior positions.
* **Computer Science graduates** dominate senior-level roles and earn the highest starting salaries.
* **Business graduates** dominate overall employment but are underrepresented in senior roles.
* Several fields (Arts, Education, Nursing, Marketing, Psychology, Finance) show **no senior-level representation**, indicating slower career progression.
